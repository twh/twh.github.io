I'm a Senior HPC engineer with 20 years of experience in large-scale computing infrastructure. My career spans high-energy physics computing at Caltech/CERN, oncology research computing at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center, and foundational model training at Paige.AI and Tempus.

Currently I manage GPU infrastructure at [Tempus](https://www.tempus.com), operating a dynamic multi-cloud compute environment that executed 1.5M Slurm jobs in 2025 and manages 7 PB of whole-slide pathology imaging data. In September 2025, Tempus acquired Paige.AI in an $81M deal — I came over as part of that acquisition and have continued scaling the infrastructure through the transition to GCP. The compute environments I built underpinned the development of [Virchow2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.00738) and [PRISM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.10254), two groundbreaking histopathology foundation models.

Before oncology AI, I was the principal administrator of the Caltech CMS Tier-2 — a 7,300-slot HTCondor cluster with 4.5 PB of storage supporting the Compact Muon Solenoid experiment at the Large Hadron Collider. Earlier I held systems engineering roles at Duke University and FedEx.
